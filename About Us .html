About Us

The iPhone is a line of smartphones made by Apple Inc. that combines personal computing, digital camera, music player, and mobile phone technology. Following over two years of research and development, the gadget made its US debut in 2007. Following that, the iPhone was made available in Europe in 2007 and Asia in 2008. The Mac OS X operating system, which gained popularity on the company's desktop computers, was intended to run on Apple's first mobile smartphone. The touch-sensitive multisensor interface of the device was its most innovative feature. Rather than using a stylus or physical keys, users could operate all programs and phone functions with their fingertips thanks to the touchscreen.
